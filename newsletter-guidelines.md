# Newsletter Content Guidelines
## Operator-Approved Rules for Every Issue

### DEFAULT Flag Protocol
Any claim flagged DEFAULT must be handled one of three ways
before the issue is approved for publication:

1. REMOVE the specific claim entirely
2. REFRAME as clearly projected:
   "Based on bundling patterns we have observed..." (T3 INFERRED)
3. REPLACE with a genuine operator insight without a fabricated number

### The 30% Claim — Permanent Resolution
The "habit tracker sales rose 30%" anecdote was flagged DEFAULT.
It was a fabricated example. It must never appear in published issues.

Replace the human moment formula with:
"Share the real vision behind TheNeuralVault —
the story of a man with a dream, a phone, and a belief
that human-AI collaboration can build something
most people would call impossible."

That story is T1 VERIFIED. It is real. It is yours.
And it is more powerful than any fabricated statistic.

### Subject Line Selection Guide
- Cold audience (new subscribers): curiosity-led
- Warm audience (engaged subscribers): benefit-led
- Re-engagement: question-led

### Call to Action Rules
- One CTA per issue. Never more.
- CTA must connect to next week's content
- Reply CTAs build engagement signals on Beehiiv
- Product CTAs only after trust is established (issue 4+)

### Brand Voice Checklist Per Issue
- [ ] No hype words: magic, effortless, guaranteed, plug-and-play
- [ ] Provenance tags on all factual claims
- [ ] DEFAULT flags resolved before publishing
- [ ] Human moment is real, not fabricated
- [ ] Subject line is a promise kept inside the issue
